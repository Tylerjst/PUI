Voter Roll Purges

Since 2013, 13 states have purged eligible or inactive voters from their databases in violation of federal law.1 Some of these voters were incorrectly purged and later restored.

In Georgia, 1.6 million people -- a little over 10% of the state's registered voters -- were removed from voting rolls from 2010 to 2018. In comparison, 19 cases of voter fraud were found in Georgia in the past 20 years; only 7 were confirmed.2 Bryan Kemp won Georgia's 2018 gubernatorial election by 55,000 votes.3
