Voter ID Laws

17 states require photo ID to vote.

According to the New York Times, low-income and people of color are less likely to possess a photo ID.1Further, states are allowed to pick and choose which forms of photo ID are acceptable and which are not. In Texas, you can vote with a gun licensce but not a student ID.2

3 states feature strict non-photo ID policies. This means that if someone shows up without appropriate ID, they can vote on a provisional ballot, but their vote won't count unless they take additional steps.3 In some states, this means traveling back to the polling location with proper ID.

As if it wasn't hard enough getting to the polls the first time.
